# 2023-WSL-Opening-Rounds
Performance, Consistency, and Advancement Insights
This project analyzes the World Surf League (WSL) 2023 Opening Rounds to understand how surfers performed across heats, how consistent they were between waves, which events proved most difficult, and how often surfers advanced or were sent to elimination rounds. The goal is to highlight patterns in scoring, wave strategy, and competitive outcomes using a clean, structured Tableau dashboard.

Project Overview
The analysis focuses on four core questions:
- Who delivered the strongest opening‑round performances?
- Which surfers showed the highest consistency between waves?
- How does wave count relate to total scoring potential?
- Which events were the most difficult, and how did surfers advance across the season?

The dashboard presents these insights through a series of structured visualizations organized into thematic sections.

Dataset
The dataset was compiled from publicly available WSL heat results for the 2023 season. It includes:
- Surfer names
- Individual wave scores
- Total heat scores
- Event names
- Advancement outcomes
- Wave count per heat

Data was cleaned and prepared in Excel before being imported into Tableau.

Key Insights
Heat Performance
- John John Florence and Caroline Marks recorded the highest opening‑round scores of the season.
- Several surfers consistently produced high heat totals across multiple events.
Wave Strategy
- Surfers with higher wave counts tended to achieve higher average total scores, suggesting more attempts can increase scoring opportunities.
- The relationship between high‑scoring waves and low‑scoring waves shows a positive trend, indicating that surfers who land big waves also tend to maintain a strong baseline.
Event Difficulty
- Events such as Billabong Pro Pipeline and Hurley Pro Sunset Beach produced lower average heat scores, indicating more challenging conditions.
Advancement Outcomes
- Filipe Toledo, John John Florence, and Italo Ferreira advanced the most frequently.
- Caitlin Simmers and Matthew McGillivray were among the surfers most often sent to elimination rounds.


Tools Used
- Excel for data cleaning and preparation
- SQL for analysis
- Tableau for visualization and dashboard design
